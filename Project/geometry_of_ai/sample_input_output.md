## 5. Observations

- At k=1 and k=3, Euclidean and Manhattan distance agree on every test point.
- At k=5, the two metrics diverge on T3 (Lemon → Banana) and T6 (Lemon → Banana for
  Manhattan only), showing that neighborhood composition — and thus the majority
  vote — becomes metric-sensitive as k grows and boundary points enter the
  neighbor set.
- All experiments complete in well under 1 ms, consistent with a brute-force k-NN
  over an 18-point training set.
